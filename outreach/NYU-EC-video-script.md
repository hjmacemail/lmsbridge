# LMS Bridge — 5-minute pitch video script

**Format:** ~5:00 max. Talk to camera; cut to a screen-recording for the demo beat. Aim for ~700–760
spoken words. Practice to land under 5:00 with ~10s of slack. "**[ON SCREEN]**" = what the viewer
sees; the rest is what you say.

---

### 0:00 – 0:25 · Hook + who you are
**[ON SCREEN: you, to camera; title card "LMS Bridge"]**

"In a hard STEM course, the student who misunderstands one idea in week three usually fails in week
nine — not because they're not capable, but because nobody caught the gap in time. I'm Hasan
Aljabbouli, from NYU, and I built LMS Bridge to catch those gaps the moment they happen."

### 0:25 – 1:05 · The problem and its cost
**[ON SCREEN: simple stat cards — "25%+ DFW", "1% of courses → ⅓ of failing credits"]**

"Introductory STEM courses are gatekeepers. More than a quarter of students drop, fail, or withdraw
from courses like intro chemistry. Just one percent of courses generate about a third of all failing
credits — and it pushes out underrepresented students the hardest. Every one of those is lost
tuition, a hit to retention, and a widened equity gap. The maddening part: the learning-management
system already records every quiz result. The data exists. But it's used to look backward, never to
intervene in the moment a student is actually struggling."

### 1:05 – 2:05 · The solution + the demo
**[ON SCREEN: screen recording — student misses a quiz question → guided session opens]**

"LMS Bridge turns that data into instant, guided tutoring. Watch: a student misses a question on
inheritance. Instead of just a red 'X', LMS Bridge detects the underlying misconception and opens a
short, guided session — a Socratic question, a retrieval-practice problem, a worked correction. Here's
the key: it never gives the answer. It makes the student reason their way back. That single
constraint is why faculty actually allow an AI tutor in their course. The student recovers, and the
instructor's dashboard shows who was at risk and who bounced back — automatically."

### 2:05 – 2:45 · Why it adopts easily + why now
**[ON SCREEN: logos — Canvas, Moodle, Brightspace, Blackboard; then "or standalone — no IT"]**

"It runs two ways. One registration plugs it into any major LMS — Canvas, Moodle, Brightspace,
Blackboard. Or an instructor runs our standalone mode, shares a join code, and starts in minutes with
no IT at all. Why now? Large language models finally make per-student tutoring affordable, the LTI
standard makes one integration reach every LMS, and universities are under real pressure to improve
retention. The adaptive-learning market is already a few billion dollars and growing around twenty
percent a year."

### 2:45 – 3:30 · Business model + go-to-market
**[ON SCREEN: "Free & open-source" → "Paid: managed hosting + institution add-ons"]**

"The model is open-core. The software is free and open-source — that's our trust and distribution
engine. We make money when an institution wants it hosted, secured, and supported for them, plus
add-ons like single sign-on, admin analytics, and SLAs. It's the GitLab and Moodle playbook. Our
go-to-market is bottom-up: a single professor adopts the free version with no approval, a small pilot
proves it works, and that evidence converts into a paid department, then a campus license."

### 3:30 – 4:10 · Differentiation + competition
**[ON SCREEN: simple matrix — vs. closed platforms / chatbots / LMS-native tools]**

"Closed adaptive platforms are expensive, locked to one system, and content-bound. Generic chatbots
aren't trusted, because they just do the homework for students. LMS Bridge is the trusted middle:
pedagogically constrained so faculty allow it, privacy-first and self-hostable so institutions clear
it, open-source so there's no lock-in, and it works inside any LMS or standalone. That combination is
hard for a closed vendor to copy without abandoning their own model."

### 4:10 – 4:40 · Traction, team, the honest part
**[ON SCREEN: "Built today: full platform" → "Next: pilots + outcome data"]**

"Here's where we are, honestly: the full platform is already built and deployable today — multi-LMS,
the standalone mode, the AI tutor, security and compliance docs. We've completely de-risked 'can we
build it.' What's next is proving adoption — design-partner pilots that produce concept-mastery data.
I'm the founder and built the product; the immediate plan is to add a technical co-founder and a
faculty pilot cohort."

### 4:40 – 5:00 · The ask + close
**[ON SCREEN: "The ask: funding + mentorship → pilots → first paying department"]**

"That's exactly what this competition can fund: pilots, a small team, and the managed-hosting offering
for our first paying customers. Fund the pilots, and we come back with mastery-gain data and our first
paying department. The vision is simple — every student in a hard course gets a patient, private guide
the moment they slip, no matter the class size, the budget, or the country. That's LMS Bridge. Thank
you."

## Recording tips
- **Energy + pace:** warm, fast, confident. Pause one beat after each stat.
- **The demo is the star** — rehearse the screen recording until the miss→recover loop is crisp; it's
  your most persuasive 60 seconds.
- **Look at the lens,** not the screen. One clean take of each section, edit together.
- **Captions on** — judges often watch muted first.
- Keep a visible timer; if you run long, trim the competition paragraph (3:30–4:10) first.
