## One-line pitch (elevator — ~20 words)
LMS Bridge turns the quiz data universities already collect into instant, AI-guided tutoring that
catches struggling STEM students before they fail.

## Two-sentence version
LMS Bridge is an open-source, AI-guided remediation layer that detects a student's misconception the
moment they miss a concept and delivers short, guided tutoring — never the answer — inside any LMS or
standalone. It's free to adopt and monetizes through managed hosting and institution-grade features,
so universities can improve retention in their hardest courses at scale.

## Brief description (~100–130 words, for "describe your idea")
In cumulative STEM courses, a small early misconception compounds until the student fails weeks later,
and large classes can't deliver concept-level help in time. LMS Bridge converts the assessment data
universities already collect into just-in-time, AI-guided tutoring: it detects the likely
misconception and runs a short, pedagogically constrained session — retrieval practice and Socratic
questioning — that guides the student to recover without ever handing over the answer. It works inside
any major LMS via one integration, or standalone with no IT. The software is free and open-source;
revenue comes from managed hosting and institution add-ons. The full platform is built today; the next
step is design-partner pilots that produce concept-mastery outcome data.

## Problem (one line)
Early misconceptions in gateway STEM courses compound into high drop/fail/withdraw rates, and the LMS
data that could catch them is used only retrospectively.

## Solution (one line)
An AI tutor, constrained to diagnose and guide rather than answer, that delivers targeted remediation
the moment a student slips — in any LMS or standalone.

## Customer / market (one line)
Universities and their STEM departments (beachhead: intro CS / data-science at US R1s), reached
bottom-up through individual faculty.

## Business model (one line)
Open-core: free, open-source software; paid managed hosting + institution add-ons (SSO, analytics,
SLAs, support).

## What makes it defensible (one line)
Pedagogical trust (won't give answers) + privacy/self-hosting + any-LMS-and-standalone reach +
open-source community — a combination closed vendors can't copy without abandoning their model.

## The ask (one line)
Funding + mentorship to run design-partner pilots, add a co-founder, and convert evidence into the
first paying department.
