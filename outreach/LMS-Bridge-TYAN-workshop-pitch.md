# Workshop pitch — "AI-guided adaptive learning for STEM, built for the Global South"

**A hands-on TYAN workshop / webinar (proposed for the TWAS Young Affiliates Network)**

## The one-line pitch

A 90-minute, hands-on session where early-career educators leave with their **own running adaptive
STEM course** — built on a free, open-source tool that needs no LMS, no IT department, and no
licensing budget.

## Why TYAN, why now

Early-career scientists across the Global South teach large foundational STEM courses with little
support for individualized feedback. LMS Bridge / Sage turns assessment results into just-in-time,
pedagogically constrained AI tutoring — and it's free and self-serve, so the usual barriers
(cost, IT, procurement) disappear. This is a practical capacity-building session, not a sales demo.

## Format

- **Length:** 90-minute webinar, or a half-day in-person workshop (TITO-style).
- **Style:** train-the-trainer — every attendee builds something live.
- **Cost:** none. Attendees need only a browser; an AI key is optional (a mock mode works for the
  workshop).

## Agenda

1. **The problem (10 min)** — how early misconceptions compound in cumulative STEM courses.
2. **Live demo (15 min)** — a student hits a gap; the AI runs a Socratic, retrieval-practice
   session; the instructor sees class-level risk.
3. **Hands-on (40 min)** — attendees create a Sage course, author a short quiz tagged to concepts,
   "take" it as a student, and watch remediation get generated automatically.
4. **The pedagogy & the evidence base (10 min)** — retrieval practice, Socratic scaffolding,
   mastery learning; it diagnoses and guides, never hands over answers.
5. **Privacy, FERPA & self-hosting (5 min)** — your data, your AI key, your control.
6. **Run a pilot + Q&A (10 min)** — how to take it back to your department; forming a pilot cohort.

## Outcomes for attendees

- A working class they can use with real students immediately.
- A playbook for a small evidence-generating pilot.
- Membership in a South–South cohort sharing results.

## What we ask of TYAN

- Host/co-organize and promote the session to members.
- Optionally, after the session, **offer the tool to members** — e.g. host a TWAS-branded instance
  and/or list it as a member resource (see the pilot proposal). No funding is requested.

## What we provide

- Facilitation, materials, and a sandbox demo instance.
- Open-source software and full documentation (install guides, security/compliance summary).
- Follow-up support for attendees who want to run a pilot.
