## Which track to enter — recommendation: New Venture Competition (NVC)

I earlier framed this as "Technology Venture." After checking the actual tracks, **NVC is the
better home**, for two reasons: it is **industry-agnostic and explicitly includes "for-profit social
impact ventures"** — which is exactly LMS Bridge's profile (mission-driven software business) — and
it carries a **$75,000** prize. The Technology Venture Competition (TVC) skews toward deep-tech / IP
commercialization, which is a less natural fit for application software. **Enter NVC; keep TVC as a
fallback only if advised otherwise at the info session.**

## Eligibility checklist (confirm exact rules on the live form)

- [ ] At least one team member is a **current NYU student or eligible recent alum** (you — `@nyu.edu`). ✓ likely
- [ ] The venture is **early-stage** and hasn't exceeded any prior-funding cap the rules set.
- [ ] The venture **hasn't already won** this competition in a prior year.

## Step-by-step

1. **Now (pre-cycle):** finalize the Concept Summary and intake answers; record the 5-min video; line up a co-founder / faculty design partner if possible (a team reads stronger than a solo founder).
2. **~October:** application opens → register as Team Lead, select **NVC**, complete the Eligibility Quiz + Intake Form.
3. Upload the **Concept Summary** and **video**; submit before the **~mid-January** deadline (the 2025–26 deadline was **Jan 20, 2026, 2:00 PM ET**).
4. **Semi-finals/finals:** present the **deck** live; use the mentoring to sharpen.

## One honest gap to close before submitting

The strongest single improvement is **evidence + a team**. Solo founders with zero users can still
advance on a strong concept and a credible plan, but judges reward (a) a forming team and (b) any
real traction. If you can recruit even one co-founder/advisor and run a small pilot before the
deadline, do it — it materially raises your odds.
