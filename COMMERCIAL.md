## What this means for you

**Any institution — of any size — may download, self-host, modify, and use LMS Bridge for
free.** There is no per-student fee and no size limit. You bring your own AI provider key,
so the software is free and your AI usage runs under your own contract.

In return, AGPL-3.0 asks one main thing: **if you modify LMS Bridge and run it as a network
service, you must make your modified source code available to its users.** Using it
unmodified, or modifying it for your own internal use, places no source-sharing obligation
on you beyond the standard license terms. Internal university deployments are unaffected.

## Commercial license & support (optional)

The AGPL is the right fit for almost everyone. You may want a separate arrangement if you:

- want to **build a proprietary product** on top of LMS Bridge without AGPL's source-sharing
  obligations (a **commercial/OEM license** is available — i.e. dual licensing); or
- want **managed hosting**, an **SLA**, priority support, security/compliance assistance
  (HECVAT/VPAT/DPA), or custom integration work.

These are services and license options offered *on top of* the free software — the free
AGPL version is always available to everyone.

## Why AGPL

- Free for every institution to self-host — maximizes adoption and trust, and is the same
  license family as Moodle, the LMS many of you already run.
- Keeps the project open: anyone who improves it and offers it as a service must share their
  improvements back to the community.
- Preserves a sustainable path (dual licensing + support) without charging schools to use it.
